# COMPETITOR ANALYSIS

Competitors analyzed: Otter.ai, Fireflies.ai, Fathom, Notion AI, Microsoft Copilot, Google Gemini

## Competitor Summaries

### Otter.ai
- Overview: Transcription and collaborative notes.
- Features: Real-time transcription, speaker identification, highlights.
- Strengths: Mature transcription; mobile apps.
- Weaknesses: Limited deep semantic extraction.
- Pricing: Freemium + paid tiers.
- AI Capabilities: Transcription-focused.
- Target Audience: Students, professionals, SMBs.

### Fireflies.ai
- Overview: Meeting assistant focused on capture and search.
- Features: Transcription, keyword search, action tagging.
- Strengths: Integrations, playback features.
- Weaknesses: Limited generative summarization.
- Pricing: Freemium + paid tiers.
- Target Audience: Sales, PMs.

### Fathom
- Overview: Lightweight meeting capture and highlights for sales.
- Features: Highlights extraction, meeting clips.
- Strengths: Easy sharing.
- Weaknesses: Narrow scope.
- Pricing: Paid tiers.
- Target Audience: Sales teams.

### Notion AI
- Overview: Knowledge workspace with AI features.
- Features: Embeds AI for docs and databases.
- Strengths: Flexible knowledge management.
- Weaknesses: Generic AI not specialized for meeting extractions.
- Pricing: Subscription tiers.
- Target Audience: Teams managing documentation.

### Microsoft Copilot
- Overview: AI integrated across Microsoft 365.
- Features: Meeting recaps, action items in Teams.
- Strengths: Deep Microsoft integration.
- Weaknesses: Enterprise lock-in, licensing complexity.
- Pricing: Enterprise-level licensing.
- Target Audience: Microsoft 365 enterprises.

### Google Gemini
- Overview: Generative AI in Google Workspace.
- Features: Summaries, drafts in Docs/Meet.
- Strengths: Native Workspace integration.
- Weaknesses: Vendor lock-in for non-Google shops.
- Pricing: Enterprise plans.
- Target Audience: Google Workspace customers.

## SWOT Analysis (Our Product)
- Strengths: Hybrid AI+fallback, focused action & decision extraction, auditability.
- Weaknesses: New entrant; initial integration scope.
- Opportunities: Enterprise compliance, education sector, consultants.
- Threats: Big platform incumbents integrating similar features.

## Gap Analysis
- Gaps in market: Lack of fallback solutions; weak decision provenance; incomplete enterprise governance.
- Our advantage: Deterministic fallback, explainability, structured follow-up workflows.
